Scoring guide

Likelihood: 1 = rare, 2 = unlikely, 3 = possible, 4 = likely, 5 = almost certain. Consequence: 1 = negligible, 2 = minor, 3 = moderate, 4 = major, 5 = catastrophic. Risk score = likelihood x consequence. Low: 1–5. Moderate: 6–11. High: 12–19. Extreme: 20–25.

Quality check

Are all five risks specific to your practice business — not generic descriptions?

Do your five risks span at least three different categories?

Have you scored likelihood and consequence independently — not just assigned a number that feels right?

Are your two mitigation actions implementable in the next four weeks — not aspirational statements?

Did you resist the temptation to score everything low? A register full of low scores is almost certainly optimism bias.
