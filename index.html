Scoring guide — three separate inputs

Each risk requires three separate scores. These are explained in full in Topic 8.3 on the Week 8 CloudDeakin page, including the colour-coded matrix and a worked example. Review that page before completing this activity.

  • Likelihood (1–5) — how probable is this risk in the next 12 months?
    1 = rare   2 = unlikely   3 = possible   4 = likely   5 = almost certain
  • Consequence (1–5) — how serious would the impact be if it occurred?
    1 = negligible   2 = minor   3 = moderate   4 = major   5 = catastrophic
  • Risk score — calculated automatically as Likelihood × Consequence.
    1–5 = Low (green)   6–11 = Moderate (amber)   12–19 = High (orange)   20–25 = Extreme (red)

Quality check

Are all five risk descriptions specific to your practice business -- not generic?

Do your five risks span at least three different categories?

Have you scored likelihood and consequence independently -- not just assigned a number that feels right?

Are your mitigation actions for the two highest-scoring risks implementable in the next four weeks?

Did you resist scoring everything low? A register full of green rows is almost certainly optimism bias.
